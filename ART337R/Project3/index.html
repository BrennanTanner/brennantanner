Introduction

In this project, we worked with employees and officials from FamilySearch to create a prototype FamilySearch app for kids. In this case study, I will go over our initial brainstorming, Iteration design, and final product.

Problem to be Solved

We wanted to create an app to get kids involved with family search and create a segway for children to get into deeper and more complicated Family Search topics.This would eventually lead to a more personal connection with the gospel and a sense of identity and responsibility.

Goals

  • Prepare kids to go to the Temple
  • Show kids that Family Search is fun
  • Make them feel like a child of Heavenly Father
  • Give kids a sense of self sufficiency and responsibility
  • Strengthen and build eternal families

Initial Research

  • The Root Questions

    What’s the best way to capture and share memories?
    Why is Family Search interested in getting memories from kids?

Three Directions

Based on our research, we felt there were three directions to best solve the solution and appeal to kids. We divided ourselves into three groups to better explore these different directions.

Game

Kids react well to task and reward systems. They enjoy daily tasks with rewards, customizable space, a personal avatar, Interesting gameplay/mechanics.

Social

Social media is a large part of youth culture, and so we wanted to create an instagram/facebook type platform where kids can share events in their lives and connect with living relatives.

Memories

We learned that kids love to take pictures of everything. This is a great way for them to record their history. We thought a space for them to upload their memories and write about them would help them develop journaling skills.
