Introduction

The Fittur app

In this project I chose an ugly app with good intentions and redesigned it from the ground up. This makeover process was a study on crafting a pleasent and intuitive user experience while still paying homage to the original app design.
In this case study I will go over the entire process I used to recreate the Fittur Fasting app.

Initial Research

  • Choosing an app

    I initially looked through the app store for any apps that stuck out to me, and oh boy did Fittur stick out like a sore thumb. There were a few others I debated on, but ultimately the Fittur Fitness app was the easy choice.

  • Look at all those colors.
  • The fasting app is just a timer, the circle isnt even useful.
  • Very bare-bones.

  • Fittur offers a large number of services on their platform, so I decided to focus on just the fasting tool in order to keep it simple.

  • K.I.S.S

    I decided to follow the age old adage, K.I.S.S:"Keep It Simple Stupid". So as I researched I looked at a lot of fasting/fitness apps that tracked diet and kept the information easy to digest. I found a LOT of apps, but only a few that were in the direction that I wanted to go. I took notes and downloded a couple just to play with the UI.


  • Here is my favorite app I found during research

  • Tracking system to measure progress
  • I liked how it split the timer into sections
  • Meal tracker is good
  • badges are neat but not nessecary
  • A page with real medical information. This is my favorite part

  • General direction

    I now had several ideas and had drawn up the first early iterations (Images in the next section). I only needed now to decide on the direction I wanted to take and start the work to turn this gross app into what I was imagining in my head. Soon I would get my first working paper prototype.

  • Usability Testting

    Usability testing was my number one source for development changes. All together I performed 9 different usability tests, all with different people.



  • Scenarios

    I used 3 different scenarios in each test. Mainly:

    1. Opening the app and starting a fast.
    2. Changing the length of the fast
    3. Seeing what the meals are for today.
